03

The questions you won't ask out loud

How do I actually…

Things upperclassmen wish they'd known sooner.

№ 01

The first key assist per semester is free. After that, they're $20 each.

№ 02

Sophomore-summer finance recruiting starts fall of sophomore year. If that's your path, treat freshman year as prep.

№ 03

CPS drop-ins are actually drop-in. You don't need an appointment to be seen the same day.

№ 04

The swim test is real. So is the PE requirement. Knock them out early — fewer headaches later.

№ 05

Use Flex at the off-campus grocery and pharmacy partners. It's basically money.

№ 06

Your CSA advisor can help with registration holds, AP credit issues, and most administrative obstacles. Email them.

№ 07

Butler is open 24/7 during the semester. Use it well — and remember to sleep.

№ 08

Get on the Public Safety text alert system via SSOL. You'll know about closures, weather, and incidents first.

Suggestions, not scripts

Roadmaps.

These are sketches, not playbooks. Every upperclassman you ask will give you slightly different advice — and most of them will be right, just for themselves. There's a saying worth holding onto: the law of equal and opposite advice. For any solid piece of guidance, you can find someone equally smart giving the opposite. "Recruit early." / "Don't recruit yet — explore." Both can be true, depending on the person. Use these as starting points. Talk to many people. Trust your own read.
